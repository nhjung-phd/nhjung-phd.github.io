About

AX Nexus Lab studies how modern AI models can be connected to explicit decision logic for reliable use in applied environments. The lab is particularly interested in systems that remain interpretable, reproducible, and deployment-aware, especially in domains where decision quality matters as much as predictive accuracy.

The lab’s work spans forecasting architectures, efficient representation learning, constrained decision pipelines, and real-world applications in finance, optimization, and operational analytics.

Research Themes

Time-Series Forecasting

  • Forecasting architectures for structured temporal data
  • Long-horizon evaluation and robust backtesting
  • Multi-scale and hierarchical temporal representation learning

Efficient AI Systems

  • Compression, quantization, and distillation
  • Lightweight inference and deployment-aware design
  • On-device and resource-constrained AI settings

Decision Systems

  • Constraint-aware decision pipelines
  • Training-free and reproducible LLM-based reasoning systems
  • Bridging AI outputs with explicit optimization logic

Applied Domains

  • AI-driven finance and asset allocation
  • Operational decision-making and risk monitoring
  • Applied analytics for real-world forecasting tasks
